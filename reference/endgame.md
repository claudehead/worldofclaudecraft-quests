# Endgame: Cap, Virtual Levels & Prestige

The level cap is **20**. Reaching it takes **167,200 total XP**. After that your character keeps progressing in two ways — cosmetic **virtual levels** and meaningful **prestige ranks** — and **rested XP** speeds the whole climb.

## At the cap
- Levels 1–20 use the normal XP curve (the last level, 19→20, alone costs **21,300 XP**).
- Once you hit the cap, **every XP you earn is banked as lifetime XP** — nothing is wasted. That banked XP drives both systems below.

## Virtual levels (cosmetic)
- Past the cap your XP bar keeps "leveling" into a **virtual level**, purely for show, up to **200**.
- Each virtual level past the cap costs **~10% more** lifetime XP than the last, so the bar slows down the higher you push it.
- It does not change your power — it is a long-term progress/flex meter.

## Prestige ranks
- **Every 23,200 lifetime XP earned *past the cap* = one prestige rank** (that's a full level-cap bar's worth per rank).
- You can prestige only **at level 20**, and only once you've banked enough post-cap XP for the next rank.
- Because rank is a pure function of XP *actually earned* past the cap, it can't be cheesed — you simply keep playing at cap and the ranks accrue.
- **Tip:** at cap, the fastest prestige is the same as fast leveling — chain level-appropriate quests and high-XP content rather than grinding low mobs.

## Rested XP (use it the whole way up)
- Rested XP **banks while you're resting at an inn** (standing in/just beside an inn, out of combat).
- It fills at **~5% of a level per 8 in-game hours** and **caps at 1.5 levels** of banked XP.
- Spend it by going out and killing — banked rested XP is consumed as **bonus kill XP** until the pool runs dry.
- **Habit:** log out / idle inside an inn. You'll come back with a rested pool that makes your next session's grind noticeably faster — all the way to cap and beyond.
