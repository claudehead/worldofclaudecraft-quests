# Heroic dungeons & the Heroic Quartermaster 🛡️

v0.23 adds a **Heroic** difficulty to all four 5-player dungeons — the same layouts, retuned harder, dropping **Heroic Marks** you spend on the game's only source of **neck and ring jewelry**.

> 🛡️ **The loop:** clear a dungeon on Heroic → each dungeon drops **1 Heroic Mark a day** off its final boss → save up and buy epic rings (12 marks) and necklaces (16 marks) from **Quartermaster Vex** in Highwatch.

---

## What Heroic difficulty is

Heroic re-runs a dungeon with its mobs **pinned to level 22** — two levels above the level-20 player cap — hitting much harder. Same bosses, same layout, tuned for a well-geared group. There are **no prerequisites**: just select **Heroic** on the difficulty picker before you enter. (It's gear-gated by design rather than by a hard item-level lock.)

The four Heroic dungeons:

| Dungeon | Final boss |
|---|---|
| **The Hollow Crypt** | Morthen the Gravecaller |
| **The Sunken Bastion** | Vael the Mistcaller |
| **The Drowned Temple** | Ysolei |
| **Gravewyrm Sanctum** | Korzul the Gravewyrm |

---

## Heroic Marks

- On a Heroic clear, **every eligible member** (including anyone downed at the end) loots **1 Heroic Mark** from the **final boss** — personal loot, no rolling or competition.
- **Daily gate:** each character can earn a mark from a given Heroic dungeon **once per UTC day**. Run all four and that's **up to 4 marks per day**.
- Marks are stackable inventory items; you can't vendor them for copper.

**Lockouts** land on the *kill*, not the door: once you've cleared a Heroic dungeon you can't claim a fresh Heroic run of it until the daily reset — though you can still corpse-run back into your party's live instance. Normal and Heroic keep **separate lockouts**, so a Normal clear never burns your Heroic run.

## Quartermaster Vex — the jewelry vendor

The **Heroic Quartermaster**, **Quartermaster Vex**, stands in **Highwatch** (Thornpeak Heights, the level-20 zone). Every piece is **Epic, level 20, item level 26**, carries no armor type (any class can wear any piece), and is bought with Heroic Marks.

### Rings — 12 Heroic Marks each

| Ring | Stats |
|---|---|
| Seal of the Nine Oaths | +7 Strength, +4 Stamina |
| Sutil's Gambit | +7 Agility, +4 Stamina |
| Niela's Coldlight Band | +7 Intellect, +4 Stamina |
| Oath of the Round Table | +5 Strength, +6 Stamina |
| Zyzz's Deathless Signet | +5 Intellect, +6 Spirit |
| The Architect's Cornerstone | +6 Intellect, +5 Spirit |

### Necklaces — 16 Heroic Marks each

| Necklace | Stats |
|---|---|
| Swiftfang Talisman | +6 Strength, +6 Agility |
| Yumi's Keepsake Locket | +7 Agility, +5 Stamina |
| Zense Meridian | +7 Intellect, +5 Spirit |
| Medallion of Endless Profit | +7 Strength, +5 Stamina |

> 💍 **Why it matters:** jewelry slots have no other source in the game, so Heroic is the *only* way to fill your neck and both rings with epics. Pick by stat identity — Strength for warriors/paladins, Agility for rogues/hunters, Intellect/Spirit for casters and healers.

## Getting started

1. Gear up on **Normal** dungeon and quest rewards until you can hold your own at level 20.
2. Form a five, pick **Heroic** on the door, and clear.
3. Loot your **Heroic Mark** from the final boss.
4. Rotate through **all four dungeons daily** — that's four marks a day toward a full jewelry set.
5. Spend at **Quartermaster Vex** in Highwatch: budget **~36 marks** for three rings, **16** per necklace.
