Specializations — signature abilities & masteries 🎯

v0.25 reworks talents. Picking a specialization is now a real choice with real teeth: the moment you choose one, you're handed a signature ability unique to that spec and a passive identity mastery that scales as you level. Every one of the 27 specs (three per class) has both.

🎯 The basics: at level 10 you unlock talents and pick a spec. That instantly grants your spec's signature ability (no point to spend, no node to reach) and switches on its identity mastery passive, which grows from 50% strength at level 10 to full strength at level 20. Then you spend your talent points — all 11 of them by cap — inside that spec's tree.

Signature abilities

A signature is a full ability your spec grants the instant you select it — no talent point, no gated node. Twenty-four of the 27 are brand-new abilities shipped with this update; the other three reuse an existing spell. A few:

Identity masteries

Your spec's mastery is a passive that defines how it plays. It applies purely from picking the spec and scales linearly with level — half strength at level 10, full strength at 20. Some examples:

  • Sharpened Blades (Warrior · Battlecraft) — +15% melee ability damage, +25% physical crit damage
  • Kindled Faith (Paladin · Sacrament) — your healing crits heal for double
  • Packbond (Hunter · Packlord) — pet deals +35% damage and has +8% health
  • Brittlebreak (Mage · Cryomancy) — +25% Frostbolt/Frost Nova damage, +10% armor
  • Fiendlore (Warlock · Pactbound) — 20% of damage you take is redirected to your demon, +10% Stamina
  • Primal Heart (Druid · Wildfang) — +15% physical & bleed damage, +20% threat

What changed

Before, your "spec" was just wherever you'd sunk the most points. Now it's an explicit choice that pays off immediately — signature + mastery before you spend a single point. Points spent in another spec's tree no longer do anything (off-spec nodes go dormant), so builds are cleaner and more committed.

Respeccing re-picks the spec and reallocates every point, swapping your signature and mastery to match. Choose the spec that fits how you want to play — the identity is the point.

See also: the class guides for full ability lists, the DPS calculator and tier lists which model these masteries, and The Book of Deeds — spending all 11 talent points is itself a deed.
