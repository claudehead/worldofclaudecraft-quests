Professions & the crafting endgame 🔨

v0.23 lands the professions endgame: three gathering skills, ten crafts arranged on a ring, ten archetypes that say who your character is, and a crafting hub at Highwatch. This is the shape of the system — exact reward numbers are still being tuned.

🔨 The shape: gather raw materials (Mining / Logging / Herbalism) → level a craft from 0 to 300 → specialize at skill 75 for discounts → declare an archetype that defines your identity → craft at the Highwatch hub (level 20).

Gathering

Three gathering professions, tracked independently:

  • Mining — ore and stone from veins
  • Logging — timber from trees
  • Herbalism — herbs and plants

Proficiency builds through successful harvests and shifts your output toward rarer grades (common → uncommon → rare → epic → legendary) — though the very rarest grades stay rare no matter how skilled you are.

Tools matter. Vendors sell basic gathering tools; better ones are crafted, and higher-tier tools unlock richer nodes and work them faster over a long session. You can slot tool effects onto them:

Tool effect Does
Gatherer's Cache Extra quantity per node
Artisan's Eye Higher material quality
Quickening Charm Faster node respawns

Each effect starts with 20 durability charges, spent faster against equal-or-higher-rarity targets and barely at all on low-rarity ones.

The ten crafts

Every craft runs its own recipe list, with skill from 0 to 300. Basic recipes are open to everyone from the start; more advanced ones unlock as your skill climbs (the tier-3 boundary sits at skill 75). The ten crafts sit in a fixed ring, and where a craft sits matters — neighbours share recipes and mechanics, and each craft has an opposite five seats away.

# Craft # Craft
1 Armorcrafting 6 Cooking
2 Weaponcrafting 7 Inscription
3 Jewelcrafting 8 Enchanting
4 Alchemy 9 Tailoring
5 Engineering 10 Leatherworking

Specialization (skill 75+). Hit 75 in a craft and you become specialized, unlocking two perks: a material-cost discount on that craft's recipes, and an extra recharge discount on tool effects you originally crafted. (Current values sit around 20% / 25% and may shift with tuning.)

Combination recipes. The ring supports recipes that call on two neighbouring crafts at once — rewarding players (or trading partners) who invest in adjacent trades rather than going it alone. Armorcrafting + Weaponcrafting and Alchemy + Engineering are two examples.

⚙️ Market throttles. To stop maxed specialists flooding the economy, crafting has a small copper fee scaled to an item's value and a cap of ~10 crafts per rolling 60 seconds. Craft for use and trade, not for spam.

The ten archetypes

Each craft also stands for an archetype — a broader identity beyond just working the trade. You hold one active archetype at a time; it's a statement about who your character is, recognized in how the world addresses you.

  • Choosing one: you declare your first archetype through an early Zone 1 story quest that formally accepts you into that identity. Until then you have no archetype.
  • Switching: possible but not casual — you complete a repeatable "make amends" quest for your old archetype, and each successive switch costs more, so the choice stays meaningful.

Your active archetype is what gates your crafting direction and endgame power. (The exact rewards and recognition are still being filled in — expect this to grow.)

The crafting hub — Highwatch

The endgame crafting hub is at Highwatch in Thornpeak Heights (Zone 3, the level-20 zone), reusing the existing town centre. It hosts one station for each of the ten crafts arranged around the hub, and is gated to level 20 so you can comfortably work the surrounding zone while you craft.

Getting started

  1. Pick up Mining, Logging or Herbalism and gather while you level.
  2. Feed those materials into a craft and push its skill toward 75 to specialize.
  3. Declare your archetype via the Zone 1 story quest when you're ready to commit.
  4. At level 20, base yourself at the Highwatch hub and work combination recipes with a neighbouring craft — or a trading partner who's leveled it.
